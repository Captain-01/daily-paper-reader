## 摘要
微生物培养对于理解环境微生物的生理、生态和生物技术潜力仍然至关重要，然而传统的基于平板的方法（CPM）只能回收环境微生物组中极微小的一部分。极地地区，尤其是北极土壤，代表了独特的“微生物暗物质”库，由于寡营养条件、低温和冻融循环对微生物生长施加了严重的生理限制，这些微生物仍然难以培养。在此，我们首次系统地将微流控液滴技术（MDT）应用于北极活动层土壤微生物群，并使用相同的起始细胞数、R2A培养基和15°C培养条件，将其性能与CPM进行基准比较。MDT的回收率比CPM高6.5至8.1倍，分离通量提高了180倍以上。近全长16S rRNA基因测序（PacBio）显示，MDT在所有分类水平上回收了显著更高的分类丰富度，在高细胞输入组（DropAS_H）中检测到256个属，而相应的平板组（PlateAS_H）中为211个属。值得注意的是，MDT产生了更均匀的群落分布，显著降低了如假单胞菌属和黄杆菌属等快速生长的富营养菌的优势。此外，来自MDT的约50%的序列归属于潜在的新物种（与模式菌株相似度<98.46%），27%归属于潜在的新属（相似度<95%）。通过Sanger测序进行的菌株验证确认了17个分离株中的12个为候选新物种，其中一株代表了一个属于德沃斯氏菌科（Devosiaceae）的潜在新属。这项研究表明，MDT是获取极地土壤微生物组中未培养的绝大多数微生物的有力平台，并建立了一个用于高通量分离新型冷适应细菌的流水线。

## Abstract
Microbial cultivation remains essential for understanding the physiology, ecology, and biotechnological potential of environmental microbes, yet conventional plate-based methods (CPM) recover only a minute fraction of the environmental microbiome. Polar regions, particularly Arctic soils, represent unique reservoirs of "microbial dark matter" that remain challenging to cultivate, owing to oligotrophic conditions, low temperatures and freeze-thaw cycles that impose severe physiological constraints on microbial growth. Here, we report the first systematic application of microfluidic droplet technology (MDT) to Arctic active-layer soil microbiota and benchmark its performance against CPM using identical starting cell numbers, R2A medium, and incubation at 15{degrees}C. MDT achieved 6.5- to 8.1-fold higher recovery rates than CPM and improved isolation throughput by >180-fold. Near-full-length 16S rRNA gene sequencing (PacBio) revealed that MDT recovered significantly higher taxonomic richness across all taxonomic levels, with 256 genera detected in the high-cell-input group (DropAS_H) versus 211 in the corresponding plate group (PlateAS_H). Notably, MDT yielded a more even community distribution, significantly reducing the dominance of fast-growing copiotrophs such as Pseudomonas and Flavobacterium. Moreover, approximately 50% of sequences from MDT were affiliated with potential novel species (<98.46% identity to type strains), and 27% with potential novel genera (<95% identity). Strain verification by Sanger sequencing confirmed 12 of 17 isolates as candidate novel species, among which one strain represented a potential novel genus within Devosiaceae. This study demonstrates that MDT is a powerful platform for accessing the uncultured majority of polar soil microbiota and establishes a pipeline for high-throughput isolation of novel cold-adapted bacteria.
